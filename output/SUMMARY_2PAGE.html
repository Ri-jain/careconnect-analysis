Factors, Enrichment & Recommendations

Q3: Explanatory Factors for Unmet Demand

Factor 1: Caregiver Supply per ZIP (Strongest Signal)

ZIPs with unfilled shifts average 199 active caregivers vs 313 for filled shifts — a 36% supply gap. This is the strongest operational factor. High-volume Bronx ZIPs (10463, 10462, 10473) combine heavy shift demand with below-median caregiver density.

Factor 2: Invitation Coverage

Available interaction data shows unfilled shifts receive near-zero caregiver invitations. Filled shifts receive ~1.6× more invitations on average. This indicates a routing/notification failure upstream of caregiver decision-making.

Factor 3: Day-of-Week Pattern

Friday (11.7%) unmet rate is 28% above Tuesday (9.1%). Mondays (11.0%) are similarly elevated. End-of-week and start-of-week coverage gaps suggest caregivers disengage from the platform on weekends.

Q4: Recommended Actions

1. Targeted Caregiver Recruitment in Census-Identified Supply Deserts
Cross-reference high-demand-pressure ZIPs (score>40) with low active-caregiver counts. Priority: Bronx ZIPs 10029, 10467, 10456, 10032. These combine high elderly populations (11–16%), low median incomes ($33K–$46K), and 10–11% unmet rates at massive scale (25K–29K shifts/period). Launch hyper-local recruiting at community centers, churches, and workforce programs in these ZIPs.
Track: Active caregivers per shift · Time-to-fill by ZIP · Monthly caregiver onboarding rate
2. Fix Invitation Routing — Implement Automated Escalation
Unfilled shifts receive near-zero invitations. Implement a rule: if 0 invites sent within 4hrs of shift creation, auto-widen the invite radius by 5mi and alert the coordinator. Use caregiver proximity, availability history, and discipline match to rank invitations — not broadcast blasting.
Track: % shifts with ≥1 invite within 4hrs · Invite-to-apply rate · Coordinator alert response time
3. Friday/Monday Priority Program + Medicaid ZIP Incentives
Friday (11.7%) and Monday (11.0%) are consistently hardest to fill. Badge these as "Priority Shifts" in the app. For the 22 low-income ZIPs with high unmet rates — where Medicaid reimbursement constrains pay — explore agency-funded top-up bonuses or non-monetary incentives (parking, transport stipends).
Track: Friday fill rate · Priority shift time-to-fill · Caregiver acceptance rate in low-income ZIPs
